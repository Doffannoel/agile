Agile Project Management

Course Description

This course focuses on the foundational principle of agile paradigm for developing a software. Two agile methodologies, i.e., the Kanban and Scrum methodologies, will be mainly discussed throughout the course. Students will be asked to work in a group to develop a simple, working software that follows the agile methodologies. Upon approval, the instructor can, for example, act as a stakeholder, and the students act as a scrum team. And, the scrum team will be asked to demonstrate their understandings of the agile principles. During the process, a software tool will be used to help students to collaborate and implement the agile methodologies.

Course Learning Outcomes & Lesson Learning Outcomes

Supported CPL Code CPMK Code CPMK Statement Sub-CPMK Statement
P7/X1 CPMK1 Understanding the concepts, principles, and methodology of Agile Project Management, as well as comparing it with traditional methodologies. Students are able to understand the fundamentals of Agile Project Management and differentiate Agile methodology from traditional (Waterfall) methodology.
P7/P8, K/U1 CPMK2 Applying planning, execution, and project management techniques based on Agile through simulations and case studies. Students are able to master Agile frameworks such as Scrum, Kanban, and Lean, as well as select the appropriate framework for different types of projects.
P7/X4 CPMK3 Adopting an Agile mindset and fostering a culture of collaboration, transparency, and adaptability in a team environment. Students are able to identify the Agile mindset and project risks, as well as manage changes in an Agile manner.
K/U3, K/X4 CPMK4 Evaluating the success of Agile-based projects using performance metrics, retrospectives, and continuous improvement practices. Students are able to understand and utilize Agile performance metrics such as velocity, cycle time, and burn-down charts.

Course Delivery

  • Forms of Learning (related to teaching hours): Lectures, Response Sessions, Tutorials, Seminars, or equivalent.
  • Learning Methods: Small Group Discussion, Role-Play & Simulation, Project-Based Learning.
