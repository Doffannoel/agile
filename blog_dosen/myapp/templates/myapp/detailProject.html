Erasmus+ CBHE - Interdisciplinary Approach Towards Fostering Collaborative Innovation in Food Waste Management (IN2FOOD)

Project Gallery

See More

This project intends to incorporate 21st-century skill learning into higher education curriculum in Indonesia by investigating the issue of food waste as a societal challenge. The project is funded by the EU Erasmus+ Capacity for Higher Education program. The project consortium consists of 8 universities; 3 from EU and 5 from Indonesia.

Methodology

  • Research & Analysis - Identifying gaps in curriculum and industry needs.
  • Curriculum Development - Adapting problem-based learning from European universities.
  • Implementation & Evaluation - Pilot testing curriculum and analyzing impact.
  • Industry Collaboration - Applying food waste solutions with industry partners.
  • Dissemination - Publishing research and educational campaigns.

Results

  • New curriculum implemented in 5 universities.
  • 100+ lecturers trained in innovative methods.
  • Industry partnerships & pilot projects reducing food waste.
  • Public campaign increasing awareness.
  • Research publications influencing education policy.
